## Summary

This article covers two major groups of computer components: **input devices** used to enter data, and **output devices** used to display or deliver information.

The first part covers keyboards, pointing devices (such as the mouse), pen input (styluses and graphics tablets), motion-based input, voice input, video input, as well as scanners and optical readers.

The second part discusses visual displays such as monitors and digital televisions, various types of printers (ink-jet, laser, 3D, all-in-one, and mobile), and other output devices such as headphones, data projectors, interactive whiteboards, joysticks, and game controllers.

### Notes on Each Category

- **Ergonomic keyboards** are designed to reduce wrist strain injuries during long typing sessions.
- **Optical mice** serve as the primary pointing device for navigating graphical interfaces.
- **Flatbed scanners** digitize sheet-sized documents and images.
- **A stylus on a graphics tablet** is used for drawing, digital handwriting, and precision editing.
- **Motion input** lets users control on-screen elements through contactless physical gestures.
- **Voice input** translates speech into commands or text via *speech recognition*.
- **Video input** captures moving images, typically through a webcam for conferencing or recording.
- **Optical readers** use light to recognize characters, marks, and codes on printed media.
- **RFID** uses radio signals to communicate with tags on objects without direct contact.

### Notes on Each Category

- **Digital monitors** display visual output from the computer in real time.
- **Printers** produce text and images on physical media; common variants include inkjet and laser.
- **3D printers** print three-dimensional objects layer by layer from digital models.
- **Speakers and headphones** deliver audio output for listening to music, calls, or game effects.
- **Data projectors** project the computer display onto a large screen for presentations.
- **Interactive whiteboards** combine display and touch input, widely used in educational and business settings.
- **Game controllers** such as joysticks, gamepads, and racing wheels provide haptic feedback during play.

> **Note:** Game controllers actually play a dual role — as an input device (receiving player commands) and as an output device (delivering vibration or haptic feedback to the player).
